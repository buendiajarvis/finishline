SECTION 03 // PROMPT LIBRARY

The actual prompts — free to copy.

These are the exact prompts we use in the workshop. Copy, paste, adapt. They work in ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and any other chat-based AI.

Resume Tailoring

Most Used
I'm applying for this job: [PASTE JOB DESCRIPTION HERE] Here's my current resume: [PASTE YOUR RESUME HERE] Rewrite my resume to target this specific role. Keep it truthful — don't invent experience I don't have. Reorganize and rephrase my experience to highlight what this employer cares about. Use the same keywords from the job description. Format it cleanly, ready to copy-paste.

After: remove "I am excited to", add one number, read it aloud — if it doesn't sound like you, change those parts.

Cover Letter

Essential
Write a cover letter for this job: [PASTE JOB DESCRIPTION] About me: [WRITE 2–3 SENTENCES ABOUT YOUR BACKGROUND IN YOUR OWN WORDS] Rules: - Keep it under 200 words - No buzzwords (synergy, passionate, leverage, etc.) - Open with something specific about THIS company - Close with a clear next step Write it like a thoughtful email, not a formal letter.

Then ask: "Make it shorter. Cut 30%." Then ask: "Does this sound like a human wrote it?"

Decode Real Requirements

Insight
Here's a job description: [PASTE JOB DESCRIPTION] What are they ACTUALLY looking for that isn't explicitly stated? What would make someone stand out from other applicants? List the unspoken requirements.

This reveals what the employer really cares about — often different from what the JD says.

Hidden Job Titles

Discovery
I'm a [YOUR CURRENT OR LAST JOB TITLE] with experience in [2–3 SKILLS]. What are 5 job titles I might qualify for that I wouldn't think to search for?

Opens up job categories you never considered. Often uncovers higher-paying roles.

ATS Keyword Check

Critical
Extract the top 10 keywords from this job description that an ATS (applicant tracking system) would look for. List them by importance. Then tell me which ones are missing from my resume: [PASTE JOB DESCRIPTION] [PASTE YOUR RESUME]

This is the one that makes people gasp. Most resumes pass only 4 of 10 keywords without optimization.

Interview Question Generator

Prep
I'm interviewing for this role: [PASTE JOB DESCRIPTION] Based on this job description, what are the 10 most likely interview questions? Include: - 3 technical/role-specific questions - 3 behavioral questions (tell me about a time when...) - 2 questions about my resume/background - 2 curveball questions they might throw For each question, explain what the interviewer is actually testing.

Print this list and practice out loud before every interview.

Mock Interview Practice

Showstopper
You're a hiring manager interviewing me for [JOB TITLE] at [COMPANY]. Ask me one question at a time. After I respond, give me brief feedback on my answer, then ask the next question. Start with "Tell me about yourself."

Hardest version: "Now interview me as a skeptical hiring manager who isn't sure I'm qualified."

Skills Gap Analysis

Growth
Here's my current resume: [PASTE YOUR RESUME] Here's the job I want: [PASTE JOB DESCRIPTION] What skills or experience am I missing? For each gap, tell me: 1. How big a deal is it (dealbreaker vs. nice-to-have)? 2. What free resource can I use to learn this in 2 weeks? 3. How do I address this gap in an interview without lying?

AI gives you free learning paths. Don't skip the third point — addressing the gap in an interview is a skill.

Salary Research & Negotiation

Advanced
I'm applying for [JOB TITLE] in [CITY/REGION] with [X] years of experience. What's a realistic salary range? What factors could push me toward the higher end? Give me a script for negotiating if they offer the lower end.

Always negotiate. AI gives you market data to support your ask. Never accept the first number.
