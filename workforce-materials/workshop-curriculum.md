# AI for Job Seekers — Workshop Curriculum

**Duration:** 2 hours  
**Format:** Live, hands-on, bring-your-own-device (smartphone is sufficient)  
**Prerequisites:** None. No technical background required.  
**What they leave with:** A tailored resume, a cover letter for a specific job, 5 interview questions, and a skills gap report — all generated during the session.

---

## Pre-Workshop: What to Bring

Tell attendees in advance:
1. Their current resume (digital or paper — paper works, they'll type from it)
2. 1–2 job descriptions they're interested in (printed, saved, or bookmarked)
3. A smartphone, tablet, or laptop with internet access
4. A free ChatGPT or Claude account (they can sign up during the workshop)

---

## Minute-by-Minute Run of Show

### 0:00–0:08 | OPENING: What AI Actually Is (Not Hype)

**Goal:** Demystify AI. Kill the fear. Set expectations.

**What you say:**
> "You've probably heard AI is going to take everyone's jobs. It's not. But people who know how to use AI are going to take jobs from people who don't. That's why you're here. In the next two hours, I'm going to show you exactly how to use these tools — which are free — to get hired faster than the people who aren't using them."

**Key points:**
- AI is a tool, like a calculator or a search engine — not a replacement for you
- You stay in control. AI suggests, you decide
- These tools are free (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini — all have free tiers)
- This is not a computer science class. If you can text, you can use AI

**Live demo (2 minutes):** Type a job description into ChatGPT and ask "What's this employer actually looking for?" Show the result. That's the hook.

---

### 0:08–0:30 | MODULE 1: Resume Tailoring (22 minutes)

**Goal:** Transform a generic resume into a targeted one for a specific job.

**Step 1 — The Prompt (5 min, demo):**

```
I'm applying for this job:

[PASTE JOB DESCRIPTION]

Here's my current resume:

[PASTE RESUME]

Rewrite my resume to target this specific role. Keep it truthful — don't invent experience I don't have. But reorganize and rephrase my experience to highlight what this employer cares about. Use the same keywords from the job description. Format it cleanly, ready to copy-paste.
```

**Live walk-through:** You do one together, projecting your screen. Pick a volunteer's job description and resume. Show the output. Point out what the AI did well and what needs a human fix.

**Step 2 — Hands-On (12 min):** Everyone does their own. Walk the room. Help people who are stuck.

**Step 3 — Cleanup (5 min):** The critical skill. AI resumes sound AI-generated. Teach them to:
- Remove phrases like "I am excited to" / "passionate about"
- Add one specific number or result (even an estimate)
- Read it aloud. If it doesn't sound like you, change it

---

### 0:30–0:50 | MODULE 2: Cover Letters (20 minutes)

**Goal:** Generate a cover letter that doesn't sound like AI wrote it.

**The Prompt:**

```
Write a cover letter for this job:

[PASTE JOB DESCRIPTION]

About me: [2–3 sentences about their background, in their own words]

Rules:
- Keep it under 200 words
- No buzzwords (synergy, passionate, leverage, etc.)
- Open with something specific about THIS company
- Close with a clear next step

Write it like a thoughtful email, not a formal letter.
```

**Live demo (5 min):** Show it. Then show the second version: "Make it shorter. Cut 30%."

**Hands-on (10 min):** Everyone writes theirs.

**The Polish (5 min):** Show them how to ask AI "Does this cover letter sound like a human wrote it? Be honest." Then fix based on feedback. This teaches them to use AI as an editor, not a ghostwriter.

---

### 0:50–1:05 | MODULE 3: Job Search Optimization (15 minutes)

**Goal:** Use AI to decode job descriptions, find hidden requirements, and discover related roles.

**The Prompts:**

**Decode the real requirements:**
```
Here's a job description:

[PASTE JD]

What are they ACTUALLY looking for that isn't explicitly stated? What would make someone stand out? List the unspoken requirements.
```

**Find related jobs you didn't know about:**
```
I'm a [current/last job title] with experience in [2–3 skills]. What are 5 job titles I might qualify for that I wouldn't think to search for?
```

**ATS keyword extraction:**
```
Extract the top 10 keywords from this job description that an ATS (applicant tracking system) would look for. List them with their importance. Then tell me which ones are missing from my resume:

[PASTE JD]
[PASTE RESUME]
```

**Live demo only (15 min):** This one is demo-heavy. Show all three prompts with a real job description. The ATS keyword extraction is the showstopper — people will gasp when they see what they're missing.

---

### 1:05–1:30 | MODULE 4: Interview Preparation (25 minutes)

**Goal:** Generate targeted interview questions and practice answers.

**The Prompts:**

**Generate likely questions:**
```
I'm interviewing for this role:

[PASTE JOB DESCRIPTION]

Based on this job description, what are the 10 most likely interview questions? Include:
- 3 technical/role-specific questions
- 3 behavioral questions (tell me about a time when...)
- 2 questions about my resume/background
- 2 curveball questions they might throw

For each, explain what the interviewer is actually testing.
```

**Mock interview (the killer feature):**
```
You're a hiring manager interviewing me for [JOB TITLE] at [COMPANY]. Ask me one question at a time. After I respond, give me brief feedback on my answer, then ask the next question. Start with "Tell me about yourself."
```

**Live demo (10 min):** Run the mock interview prompt live. Answer a few questions out loud so people see how it works.

**Hands-on (10 min):** Everyone generates their question list and starts a mock interview.

**Pro tip (5 min):** Show them the "devil's advocate" version:
```
Now interview me as a skeptical hiring manager who isn't sure I'm qualified. Challenge my answers.
```

---

### 1:30–1:45 | MODULE 5: Skills Gap Analysis (15 minutes)

**Goal:** Identify what they're missing and how to close the gap for free.

**The Prompt:**

```
Here's my current resume:

[PASTE RESUME]

Here's the job I want:

[PASTE JOB DESCRIPTION]

What skills or experience am I missing? For each gap, tell me:
1. How big a deal is it (dealbreaker vs. nice-to-have)?
2. What free resource can I use to learn this in 2 weeks?
3. How do I address this gap in an interview without lying?
```

**Live walk-through (10 min):** Run through one person's. Show how the AI gives actionable, free learning paths. This is huge for people with no budget for courses.

**Reality check (5 min):** Teach them the difference between "can learn this" and "need to be honest this is a gap." Frame gaps as growth areas, not disqualifications.

---

### 1:45–1:55 | CLOSING: Your AI Job Search System (10 minutes)

**Goal:** Give them a repeatable weekly workflow.

**The System:**
1. **Sunday night:** Find 5 jobs. Feed them + your resume to AI. Get 5 tailored resumes.
2. **Monday:** Apply to all 5 with AI-generated cover letters.
3. **Before each interview:** Run the mock interview prompt for 15 minutes.
4. **After each rejection:** Feed the job description + your resume to AI. Ask "What was I probably missing? What should I strengthen?"

**Resources to send:**
- Link to free ChatGPT signup
- Link to free Claude signup
- All prompts in a shareable document
- Your contact info (not FinishLine — just "Phil, AI Trainer")

---

### 1:55–2:00 | Q&A + Survey (5 minutes)

**Survey (what to track):**
- Name and email
- Current employment status
- What was most useful?
- What job titles are you targeting?
- Permission to follow up in 30 days?

**Your closing line:**
> "Here's what I want you to do: use ONE of these prompts tonight. Just one. Apply to one job with it. If it helps, come back next month and tell me. If it doesn't, tell me that too. I'll adjust the workshop. This is version one, and you're helping me make it better."

## After Each Workshop

1. **Same day:** Email the prompts document to everyone who signed in
2. **Day 7:** Send a one-line check-in: "Used any of the prompts yet? Reply with what worked or what didn't."
3. **Day 30:** Send the follow-up survey. Ask: interviews? offers? which prompt was most useful?
4. **Compile data:** Track interview rate, hire rate, and satisfaction. This is your sales deck for the next WDB.
