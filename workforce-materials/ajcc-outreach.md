# AJCC Outreach Scripts

Three formats for different situations. Use whichever fits.

---

## A. The Walk-In Script (highest success rate)

Walk into any America's Job Center. Ask for the site manager by name if you have it. If not:

> "Hi — I'm hoping to speak with the site manager or whoever coordinates workshops here. Is there someone available for a 5-minute conversation?"

**Once you're face-to-face with the manager:**

> "Thanks for making time. I'll be quick. My name is Phil — I'm an AI practitioner here in [city/region]. I've put together a free 2-hour workshop that teaches job seekers how to use free AI tools — ChatGPT, Claude — to get hired faster. Resume tailoring, cover letters, interview prep, all hands-on.
>
> I'm not selling anything. I'm not a vendor. I want to run this as a pilot at your center — no cost to you or your clients. I bring the curriculum, you provide a room with a screen. If it works, maybe we do it monthly. If it doesn't, no hard feelings.
>
> I've got a one-pager with the curriculum and outcomes I can leave with you. What's the best way to find a date on your calendar?"

**Why this works:**
- You say "I'm not selling anything" before they can ask
- "No cost to you or your clients" removes every objection
- "If it works, maybe monthly. If not, no hard feelings." — zero pressure
- The one-pager leaves a physical artifact behind after you leave

**What to bring:**
- Printed one-pager (see `one-pager.md`)
- Business card (personal — not FinishLine)
- A pen and a smile

## B. The Email (if you have their contact)

**Subject:** Free AI workshop for your job seekers — no cost, ready to go

**Body:**

> [MANAGER NAME],
>
> I'm an AI practitioner based in [city]. I've developed a free 2-hour hands-on workshop that teaches job seekers how to use free AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude) to tailor resumes, write cover letters, prep for interviews, and identify skills gaps — using nothing but a smartphone.
>
> I'd like to pilot it at your AJCC at no cost to the center or your clients. You provide a room with a projector or TV. I bring the curriculum, the handouts, and the instruction.
>
> The workshop is practical, not theoretical. Attendees leave with a tailored resume and cover letter for a real job they're targeting. I've attached a one-page overview.
>
> Happy to run a single pilot session — if it delivers value, we discuss recurring sessions. If not, no obligation and no hard feelings.
>
> Would you be open to a 10-minute call or an in-person chat to see if this fits?
>
> Thanks,
> Phil
> [phone number]
> [email]

## C. The LinkedIn Message (for WDB directors or Business Services reps)

**Subject/opener:** Quick idea for your AJCC — free AI workshops, zero risk

> Hi [NAME] — I saw you lead workforce development for [county/region]. I'm an AI practitioner in [city] and I've put together a free 2-hour workshop that teaches job seekers how to use free AI tools for resume tailoring, interview prep, and job search — all doable from a smartphone.
>
> I'm looking to pilot it at a couple of AJCCs at no cost. Your clients get a practical skill that demonstrably speeds up hiring. You get data on whether AI workshops improve placement rates.
>
> If it works, great — we scale it. If not, no loss. Happy to send the one-pager or hop on a quick call. Would this be worth a conversation?

## What NOT to Say

| Don't say | Why | Say instead |
|---|---|---|
| "I run an AI consulting firm" | Sounds like a sales pitch. The guard goes up instantly. | "I'm an AI practitioner" or "I work in AI" |
| "This could lead to job placements for my company" | Immediately disqualifies you. They think you're recruiting. | "I'm not recruiting. I'm not hiring. This is just skills training." |
| "I also do corporate training if you know anyone" | Kills trust. You're here for the job seekers, not the upsell. | Don't mention it. Let them ask. They will. |
| "I've done this for Fortune 500 companies" | Wrong room, wrong tone. These are frontline workforce people. | "I've trained hundreds of people on practical AI use" |
| "This is cutting-edge technology" | Intimidating. Their clients are intimidated enough. | "If you can text, you can use these tools." |

## Follow-Up Timeline

| When | Action |
|---|---|
| **Day 0** | Walk in or send email |
| **Day 3** | If no response, send a brief follow-up: "Just circling back — happy to answer any questions about the AI workshop. No pressure either way." |
| **Day 7** | If still no response, move to the next AJCC. Circle back to this one in 3 months with data from the centers that said yes. |
| **After first YES** | Lock the date immediately. "Great — how about [next Tuesday or Thursday] at 10 AM? That gives your clients a few days to hear about it." |

## Your First 5 Targets

Research these before you go:

1. **Nearest AJCC** — Look up the site manager's name. Walk in Tuesday morning (Mondays are chaos).
2. **Second-nearest AJCC** — Same process. If #1 says yes, mention it to #2. "We're already running this at [AJCC #1] in [city]."
3. **Your county's WDB office** — The Business Services rep. Different angle: "I'd like to eventually offer this to your employer partners too. But let me prove it with job seekers first."
4. **Local community college career center** — Same pitch, different door. Colleges have career centers that serve both students and community members.
5. **Goodwill or JVS office** — They hold WIOA prime contracts. They're always looking for workshop providers.
